## Greeting Patterns (by recipient type)

| Context | Greeting | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Court staff | "Greetings," | Always formal. No first names. |
| Opposing counsel | "Counselor," or "Greetings," | Never friendly |
| New clients | "Good morning/afternoon," | Warm, formal |
| Existing clients | First name only, "Hello {Name}," | Relaxed but professional |
| Government | "Greetings," | Mirror their formality |
| Referral sources | "Good morning," | Polite, brief |

## Closing Patterns

- **Default:** "Best," (95% of all correspondence)
- **Formal/court:** "Respectfully," (rare — only official filings)
- **To long-term clients:** "Best," (still — never changes)
- **Never uses:** "Sincerely," "Regards," "Thanks," "Cheers"

## Tone Tendencies

- Leads with action item or request — no preamble
- Rarely writes more than 5 sentences in a single email
- Uses numbered/bulleted lists for multi-item follow-ups
- Does NOT use exclamation marks
- Does NOT use emoji
- Capitalizes for emphasis (e.g., "PLEASE send the documents by Friday")
- Frequently uses "Please advise" as a closing ask
- Signs as "Rodney M. Johnson, Esq." or "RMJ" in quick replies

## Subject Line Style

- Descriptive, case-relevant: "Re: Porter Probate — Filing Deadline 7/18"
- Includes case name when relevant
- Never cute or marketing-style
- Uses "—" (em dash) as separator

## Response Time Patterns

- Court/government emails: responds within 2-4 hours
- Client emails: responds within 24 hours (often same day evening)
- Vendor/admin emails: responds within 48 hours
- Lawyer.com leads: rarely responds (low priority)

## Payment Communication Style

- Direct about fees: "My retainer for this matter is $X"
- Payment instructions always include CashApp + Zelle options
- Filing fees communicated with exact amount + receipt
- Never apologizes for billing — treats as routine business

## Common Phrases (verbatim from observed correspondence)

- "Please advise."
- "I am in receipt of your communication."
- "Please find attached..."
- "I will be filing [document] on your behalf."
- "My office will follow up with you regarding..."
- "Please contact my office at your earliest convenience."
- "I look forward to resolving this matter."
- "Thank you for your patience in this matter."
- "Your matter is currently pending [status]."
- "We are actively working on your case."

## Content Preferences

- Always states what he needs from the recipient clearly
- Never overexplains legal process to clients (assumes they'll ask if confused)
- Includes deadlines prominently when they exist
- References case numbers in correspondence with courts
- Does NOT include personal anecdotes or small talk
- Client-facing emails never discuss case strategy (privilege concern)

## Formatting Preferences

- Plain text preferred over HTML (for client emails)
- Branded template for formal communications (welcome, thank you)
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences each)
- Signature block: full name, title, firm, phone

## Do NOT (observed corrections/preferences)

- Do not use "Dear Sir/Madam" — too generic
- Do not write lengthy disclaimers at the bottom
- Do not cc other attorneys without explicit instruction
- Do not include case details in subject lines to opposing counsel
- Do not schedule without checking his calendar first
- Do not promise specific timelines to clients (say "shortly" or "within X business days")
